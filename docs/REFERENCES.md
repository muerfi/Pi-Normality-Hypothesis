# References

This page lists background sources used for the scientific framing of the
project. It is deliberately short. Additions should be real sources that were
checked, relevant, and useful for understanding normal numbers, π digit
experiments, or reproducible scientific computation.

## Normal numbers and examples

- Émile Borel, "Les probabilités dénombrables et leurs applications
  arithmétiques," *Rendiconti del Circolo Matematico di Palermo*, 27 (1909),
  247-271.
  - Introduced the measure-theoretic setting in which almost all real numbers
    are normal.
- D. G. Champernowne, "The Construction of Decimals Normal in the Scale of Ten,"
  *Journal of the London Mathematical Society*, s1-8, no. 4 (1933), 254-260.
  - Classic explicit construction of a base-10 normal number.
- A. H. Copeland and P. Erdős, "Note on Normal Numbers," *Bulletin of the
  American Mathematical Society*, 52 (1946), 857-860.
  - Gives another important construction of normal numbers.

## π and empirical normality studies

- David H. Bailey, Jonathan M. Borwein, Cristian S. Calude, Michael J. Dinneen,
  Monica Dumitrescu, and Alex Yee, "An Empirical Approach to the Normality of
  π," *Experimental Mathematics*, 21, no. 4 (2012), 375-384.
  DOI: `10.1080/10586458.2012.665333`.
  - Large-scale empirical study of π digit prefixes. Useful as an example of
    careful finite experimentation and cautious interpretation.
- David H. Bailey and Jonathan M. Borwein, "Normal Numbers," in *Surveys in
  Number Theory: Papers from the Millennial Conference on Number Theory*, 2008.
  - Survey discussion of normality and related computational questions.

## Citation policy for this repository

When adding a new claim to the documentation, prefer primary literature or a
stable mathematical reference. Avoid adding references merely because they are
popular summaries. Do not cite a source that has not been checked.
